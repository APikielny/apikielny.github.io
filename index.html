Research

I build models that advance the theoretical foundations of the field and are robust enough to ship on-device to millions of users.

Hist2Style: Histogram-Guided Stylization with Bilateral Grids
Dekel Galor, Adam Pikielny, Zhoutong Zhang, Ke Wang, Laura Waller, Jiawen Chen, Ilya Chugunov
CVPR, 2026
project page

We distill frontier image models into a small network with histogram-guided inputs and bilateral-grid outputs. Runs in real-time on consumer hardware and preserves the input image exactly by construction (no hallucinations).

Removing Reflections from RAW Photos
Eric Kee, Adam Pikielny, Kevin Blackburn-Matzen, Marc Levoy
CVPR, 2025  (Oral — top 0.7% of submissions)
project page / paper / Adobe MAX Sneaks 2023 / Adobe blog / video / Brown CS press

A realistic synthetic reflection pipeline lets a compact model remove reflections from real photographs with no real-world training data, allowing us to scale up and outperform prior works. Runs on-device in Adobe Lightroom, Photoshop, Camera Raw, and Project Indigo.

Consistent Depth Estimation for Video
Adam Pikielny, advised by James Tompkin, in collaboration with Marc Mapeke
Brown CS Senior Thesis, 2022  (1st Prize, Brown CS Undergraduate Research Symposium)
thesis

Monocular depth models applied frame-by-frame to video produce flickery, inconsistent depth. We add principled resampling and frequency modulation to an existing monodepth model to produce temporally consistent video depth.

Open Source Contributions
🌲
 pconfigs
Adobe, open source (Apache 2.0)
GitHub

pconfigs is a dataclass-based Python config library. Configs are typed, inheritable Python classes that live next to the code they configure, instead of brittle text files that drift out of sync. We used it throughout the reflection removal project. I believe strongly in readable and reproducible research and hope you'll try it in your project.
